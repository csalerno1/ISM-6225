Chicken Cacciatore

This recipe is great for a family meal or Sunday dinner! It is one my personal favorites and
a classic dish that has an abundance of flavor! Makes about 6 servings.


Ingredients

4 Pounds Cut Up Chicken Salt an Pepper (optional)
Olive Oil, 2 tbsp Butter, 2 tbsp
1 Large Onion, chopped 2 Celery Ribs, sliced
1 Large Green Pepper, sliced 1/2 Pounds Fresh Mushrooms, sliced
1 Can (28 oz) Tomatoes, drained and chopped 1 Can (8 oz) Tomato Sauce
1 Can (6 oz) Tomato Paste 1 Cup Dry Red Wine (or Water)
Dried Thyme, 1 tsp Dried Rosemary, crushed, 1 tsp
Dried Oregano, 1 tsp Dried Basil, 1 tsp
3 Garlic Cloves, minced Sugar, 1 tbsp
Grated Parmesan Cheese Pasta (Ziti is reccommended but your choice)

Kitchen Needs

Large Skillet Pan Stove
Large Pot and Large Plate Spatula
Box Grater Large Knife
Cutting Board Food Processor (optional)

Instructions

1. Dust chicken with flour. Season with salt and pepper.

2. In a large skillet, brown chicken on all sides in oil and butter
over medium-high heat. Remove chicken to platter.

3. In the same skillet, cook and stir the onion, celery, pepper and mushrooms for 5 minutes.
Stir in the tomatoes, tomato sauce, tomato paste, wine, herbs, garlic and sugar.
Bring to a boil. Reduce heat; cover and simmer for 30 minutes.

4. Return chicken to skillet. Cover and simmer for 45-60 minutes or until chicken is tender.

5. In a large pot, cook pasta of your choice.

6. Plate, serve over pasta, sprinkle with parmesan cheese, and enjoy!
